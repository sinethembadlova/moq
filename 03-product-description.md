# Product Description

## Overview

MOQ is a B2B marketplace that applies all-or-nothing crowdfunding mechanics to wholesale buying. Buyers pool funds to collectively reach a supplier's minimum order quantity, unlocking wholesale pricing and direct supplier access that no individual buyer could secure alone. If a campaign reaches its target before the deadline, the order is placed. If not, every pledge is returned. The model eliminates risk for buyers, guarantees meaningful order volumes for suppliers, and earns the platform a fee only when a transaction succeeds.

## How It Works

Before a buyer can participate in any campaign, they must register on the platform and complete a Know Your Customer (KYC) verification process. KYC establishes the trust layer that makes it safe for buyers to commit funds alongside strangers and for suppliers to accept consolidated orders from the platform.

A verified buyer funds their wallet, then browses active campaigns. Each campaign is tied to a platform-verified supplier and one of that supplier's listed deals — for example, "Stock 22-inch wigs, MOQ 1,000 units, closes in 14 days." The buyer pledges an amount from their wallet toward that campaign. A buyer can only pledge funds they have available in their wallet — no credit or overdraft is extended at the pledge stage. When a pledge is made, the pledged amount is transferred from the buyer's wallet into the campaign's wallet, a ring-fenced virtual account that holds funds on behalf of all participants until the outcome is determined.

When a campaign reaches its MOQ target before the deadline, the platform places the consolidated order with the supplier. The campaign wallet releases the funds to the supplier minus the platform's commission, and the goods are dispatched to each participant according to their pledge amount. When a campaign fails to reach its target by the deadline, the campaign wallet automatically refunds all pledged amounts back to each buyer's wallet.

Buyers who want a product not currently available in any active campaign can submit a campaign request. Campaign requests act as demand signals — the platform aggregates requests for the same product and, when sufficient interest exists, approaches the relevant supplier to onboard them and list a deal. A buyer who is interested in or pledged into an active campaign can also invite other buyers they know — particularly those in the same line of business — to join. Peer invitations help campaigns reach their MOQ targets faster and extend the platform's reach through existing trade networks.

## Supplier Participation

Suppliers enter the platform through two routes: they can register and list their deals proactively, or the platform can approach them based on accumulated demand signals from buyer campaign requests. In both cases, suppliers must complete a KYC verification process before any of their deals can be listed or campaigns can go live. Supplier KYC establishes that the business is legitimate, the products are genuine, and the supplier can fulfill orders at the stated MOQ and price.

Once verified, a supplier lists their deals — each specifying a product, MOQ, unit price, deadline window, and any relevant conditions — and launches campaigns directly from those listings. The supplier sets the terms; the platform manages the buyer aggregation, fund collection, and fulfilment coordination. This ensures every campaign is anchored to terms a supplier has already committed to, and buyers never negotiate pricing — they simply decide whether to participate.

## Revenue Model

The platform earns revenue from two sources. First, a platform fee of 3–5% is charged on each successful pledge — this fee is only collected when a campaign succeeds, meaning the platform earns nothing on failed campaigns. Second, suppliers pay a commission of 2–8% on the fulfilled order value in exchange for access to aggregated, guaranteed demand. The combined platform take is kept below approximately 10% of order value. All rates are configuration-driven so they can be adjusted per supplier, deal type, or market segment as the business scales.
