# Success Metrics

## Overview

Success for MOQ is measured across four dimensions: buyer and supplier adoption, campaign health, financial performance, and engagement quality. The metrics below define what a working platform looks like at each stage and give the team a shared definition of progress.

## Adoption

The platform's core value depends on having enough buyers and suppliers to make campaigns viable. Key adoption metrics are the number of KYC-verified buyers, the number of verified suppliers, and the number of active deals listed on the platform at any given time. Growth in verified buyers is the primary leading indicator — without buyers, campaigns cannot close and suppliers have no reason to participate.

## Campaign Health

A campaign that closes successfully is the platform's fundamental unit of value delivery. The campaign success rate — the percentage of launched campaigns that reach their MOQ before the deadline — is the most important operational metric. Alongside it, average time to campaign close indicates how quickly demand is aggregating, and the number of active campaigns at any point reflects supply-side engagement. A high campaign request volume relative to active campaigns signals unmet demand and opportunities to source new suppliers.

## Financial Performance

Gross Merchandise Value (GMV) — the total value of fulfilled orders — is the primary financial metric. Platform revenue, derived from buyer pledge fees and supplier commissions, tracks directly from GMV. Monitoring the blended take rate (combined fees as a percentage of GMV) ensures the platform stays within the target ceiling of approximately 10%. Average order value per campaign and per buyer provide additional insight into deal quality and buyer purchasing power.

## Engagement Quality

Repeat participation is the strongest signal of product-market fit. The percentage of buyers who pledge in more than one campaign, and the interval between their pledges, indicates whether the platform is becoming a habitual procurement channel. Invitation conversion rate — the percentage of buyer-sent invitations that result in a new pledge — measures the effectiveness of the peer network as a growth and campaign-closing mechanism.
